Frequently-asked Questions about Disability Statistics

What percentage of people in the United States have disabilities?

The prevelance is estimated at 12.1%. That's approximately 37.6 million people with disabilities in the US alone.

The prevelance is often much higher in developing countries, putting the worldwide average around 20%.

See http://www.disabilitystatistics.org/reports/acs.cfm?statistic=1

What is the median income of people with disabilities in the United States?

The median income for working people with disabilities in 2012 was estimated at $36,400 overall. Washington DC had the highest median, at $50,500. The median income of people without disabilities in the same year was $51,371.

See http://www.disabilitystatistics.org/reports/acs.cfm?statistic=5

What is the employment rate of people with disabilities in the United States?

The employment rate of people with disabilities is sadly quite low, at about 33.5% of those with disabilities. Some of this is due to the disability itself, and some of it is due to the lack of support or accessible infrastructure in the workplace or in society as a whole.

How many people with disabilities in the United States live in poverty?

The poverty rate is about 28.4% for people with disabilities, compared to about 12.5% of the general population in the United States.
